General Description

The Seljuk Sultanate of Rum (1077-1307 CE) was probably founded by Suleman Qotlomos after a Turkmen tribe in Syria asked him to lead them.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_11_TrRum**] The early years of the Sultanate are, however, extremely murky as it is unlikely that any local Muslim chronicles were written.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_12_TrRum**][MAJIDBENAM_BR]It is likely that the government was a largely military arrangement. The regional apparatus was organised with military officials overseeing local tax collectors. The amirs were granted land by the Sultan in return for military service [MAJIDBENAM_REF_13_TrRum**][MAJIDBENAM_BR]At his royal court a core of senior bureaucrats and scribes assisted with the central administration.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_14_TrRum**] Land, and the right to collect revenue for it, was also distributed by the Sultan to senior officials.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_15_TrRum**] These positions and the land grants often became hereditary.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_16_TrRum**][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The early 13th century was probably the high point of the Sultanate of Rum[MAJIDBENAM_REF_17_TrRum**] before Anatolia came under the authority of invading Mongols in the 1240s CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_18_TrRum**] Konya was the largest city of the polity with 30,000-40,000 inhabitants. Crusaders who reached there in 1190 reckoned it was “the size of Cologne".[MAJIDBENAM_REF_19_TrRum**]
