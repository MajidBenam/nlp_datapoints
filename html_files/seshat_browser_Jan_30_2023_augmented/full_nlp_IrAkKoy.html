General Description

The Ak Koyunlu were a loose confederation of nomadic Turkman tribes that ruled in Iran between 1339-1501 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_15_IrAkKoy] They formed an alliance with the Timurid Emirate (1370-1526 CE) until Uzun Hasan (r. c1453-1478 CE) declared himself an independent sultan.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_16_IrAkKoy] Their rule was ended by the Safavids in 1501 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_17_IrAkKoy][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The initial Ak Koyunlu government system was not complex; the sultan, a member of the Bayandor clan, was the head of a confederation[MAJIDBENAM_REF_18_IrAkKoy] and obliged to attend and abide by the decisions of a powerful council of Amirs (kengac) and tribal chiefs (boy kanlari). This collective "determined military matters and the recurrent issue of succession to the sultanate".[MAJIDBENAM_REF_19_IrAkKoy] However, by Qara Otman (c1398 CE) the Ak Koyunlu had gained "at least a rudimentary bureaucratic apparatus of the Iranian-Islamic type."[MAJIDBENAM_REF_20_IrAkKoy] in addition to support from more tribes, and better relations with Christian sedentary people.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]In the second half of the fifthteenth century, the complexity of state institutions increased another step with the conquest of eastern Iran.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_21_IrAkKoy] Uzun Hasan maintained the existing administrative system as well as their officials.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_22_IrAkKoy] Woods (1998) notes that there is evidence of an attempt to standardize and regularize administrative and financial procedures.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_23_IrAkKoy]
