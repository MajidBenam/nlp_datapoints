♠ Name ♣ sar puhi ♥ The Substitute King Ritual. The several ritual practices were undertaken by the king and the priests in order soften the gods anger caused bu king's behaviour. These included both bathing and fumigating to remove the physical and magical impurity. When the king was touched by impurity he was not able to rule properly any longer and various badlucks and catastrophes happened to his people and the kingdom. During the ceremony of the king's purification by taking the bath, the 14 prisoners participated in this act and then they were released and sent to the enemy countries. It was believed that these prisoners absorbed all evil from the king and took it with them to the king's enemies. After the ceremony of night recitation of long prayers, the king took a bath again and in seven houses he both performed rites of purification and recited prayers again in front of figurines. According to Ambos, then "In the fifth house, the exorcist stabbed a figurine of the curse-demon; the king washed his mouth with water and beer, and spat this on the figurine, which was then buried. In the sixth house, figurines of the sorcerer and sorceress were bound with a rope and buried. During that time the substitute king absorbed the evil of the eclipse and the real king was saved.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_265_IqNAssr] There are known letters from Neo-Assyrian Period saying that during the reign of Assurbanipal and Esarhadgon the substitute king ritual had been practiced.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_266_IqNAssr]

It was believed that, besides the bones of the dead, and in a mysterious but undoubted relationship with them, there existed what was called the ‘phantom’ (the ‘shade’, ‘spirit’ or ‘soul’ of our own folklore) - a kind of indistinct, hazy, insubstantial double of what the dead person had been like in life, rather as seen in dreams. Placed under the ground by the obligatory rite of burying the body, this phantom reached what would from then on be his own and final ‘home’: Hell, a sort of immense underground cavern covering the same area as our world. All the dead were gathered there, under the rule of the infernal gods, after being governed on earth by the gods of the heavens. There they led a somnolent, dull, parsimonious and mournful life.
