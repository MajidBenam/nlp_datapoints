♠ Name ♣ cattle hunt ♥ "After seven seasons of excavations, the exposed remains, the finds and the lack of certain features at Musular taken together suggest that the site was established for special activities. These activities had to do with hunting; and hunting focused mainly on cattle. [...] The big size of the wild cattle chosen for hunting, the non-domestic character of the architectural features and their similarities with the special functioning area of Asikli, and the public character of the area all make us wonder whether Musular had a symbolic meaning. The ideological role of cattle (Cauvin, 2002 ; Hodder, 1990), the possible proto-domestication status of sheep and goat versus the status of wild cattle (Buitenhuis, 1997 ; Martin et al, 2002) and selective hunting are all components of a ritual identity which, we think, endowed Musular. The reflection of such an identity would have been the post-hunting ceremonies and/or celebrations. In short, the archaeological data support the argument that Musular may have been used at certain periods for such activities." [30] The apparent ideological importance of hunting, as well as evidence that confrontations with dangerous animals was an important ritual activity in later Konya polities (specifically, Catalhoyuk) suggest that we may consider the hunt itself a ritual, that is, a customary activity that is not purely instrumental in its purpose.
