♠ Ideology forbids slavery for co-ethnics ♣ absent ♥ “I am not aware of any explicit condemnation of slavery as an institution by any ancient philosopher, nor any movement, large or small, to abolish slavery. Zenon, the founder of Stoicism, owned slaves according to his biography (by Diogenes Laertius). Both Stoics and Epicureans made much of the idea that ‘true slavery’ is not external but an internal matter: whether one is a slave to one’s desires and emotions. Thus every slave can be ‘free’ and every master can be “enslaved” depending on their character. It is true that sanctuaries sometimes owned slaves, just as they owned land or other forms of property. During the Hellenistic period, religious devotion to certain deities was conceptualized as a form of willing slavery; this is known in Ptolemaic Egypt, for example.
